## üîß **PHASE 2: REVISION STRATEGIES**

### **A. For Reducing AI Detection (Primary Goal)**

#### **1. Voice & Style Transformation**
- **Personalize the narrative**: Add first-person reflections where appropriate
  - *Instead of*: "The system implements voice interaction..."
  - *Try*: "Our team chose Sarvam AI for voice processing because..."
  
- **Vary sentence structures**: AI often uses predictable patterns
  - Mix short and long sentences
  - Use occasional rhetorical questions
  - Include transitional phrases unique to human writing

#### **2. Technical Content Humanization**
- **Add project-specific anecdotes**: 
  - "We initially struggled with Redis integration until we discovered..."
  - "During testing, we encountered unexpected latency when..."
  
- **Include authentic limitations**: 
  - "Due to time constraints, we couldn't implement..."
  - "A challenge we didn't fully resolve was..."

#### **3. Abstract & Introduction Complete Rewrite**
- **Start fresh**: Don't edit the existing text‚Äîwrite new versions from memory
- **Focus on YOUR journey**: Why THIS project? What problems did YOU face?
- **Use simpler, more direct language**

## üö® **RED FLAGS TO ELIMINATE**

**These patterns scream "AI-generated":**
- Overuse of transition words: "furthermore," "moreover," "additionally"
- Perfect but generic topic sentences
- Consistent 3-5 sentence paragraphs with identical structure
- Impersonal, omniscient narrator voice
- Excessive hedging: "may," "could," "might" without justification

## üí° **PRO-TIPS FOR AUTHENTIC ACADEMIC WRITING**

1. **Embrace slight imperfection**: Humans write slightly uneven sentences
2. **Use discipline-specific jargon** YOUR way
3. **Reference your actual tools/versions**: "We used Redis 7.2 (not just "Redis")"
4. **Include the human element**: Team decisions, debates, compromises
5. **Show don't just tell**: Instead of "The system is scalable," say "We tested with 50 concurrent users and observed..."
